# 🚦 NYC Road Safety & Collision Intelligence Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes over 238,000 traffic collision records from New York City to identify accident hotspots, injury patterns, vehicle involvement, and contributing factors.

The goal is to support data-driven road safety initiatives and public safety planning.

---

## Business Problem

Traffic collisions result in injuries, fatalities, and significant economic costs.

This project aims to:

- Identify accident hotspots.
- Analyze injury trends.
- Determine major contributing factors.
- Understand vehicle involvement patterns.
- Support road safety decision-making.

## Key Insights

### Accident Distribution

- Brooklyn recorded the highest collision volume.
- Queens followed closely behind.

### Injury Analysis

- Brooklyn and Queens recorded the highest injury counts.

### Contributing Factors

Top contributing factors included:

- Driver Inattention/Distraction
- Failure to Yield Right-of-Way
- Following Too Closely

### Vehicle Analysis

- Passenger vehicles dominated collision involvement.

### Road Hotspots

High-risk roads included:

- Belt Parkway
- Broadway
- Atlantic Avenue
- Long Island Expressway

---

## Recommendations

1. Increase enforcement in high-risk boroughs.
2. Reduce distracted driving through awareness campaigns.
3. Improve road infrastructure in accident-prone areas.
4. Strengthen traffic monitoring systems.
5. Enhance pedestrian safety initiatives.
